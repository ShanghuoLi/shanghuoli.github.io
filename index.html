I am Shanghuo Li, currently a postdoctoral researcher at the KASI. My research interests focus on molecular cloud, star and cluster formation, filament and turbulence. Mostly, I used the infrared, radio, millimeter and sub-millimeter data from, e.g., Spitzer, Herschel, ALMA, SMA, NOEMA, JVLA, IRAM 30m, JCMT, SMT, KP 12m, CSO, PMO 13.7m, KVN 21m and Tianma 65m, to probe the molecular gas and dust emission from large scale > 1 pc down to small scale < 0.001 pc.

Here are some of my completed and on going projects

Filament

Filamentary structures are ubiquitously seen in the interstellar medium, and it closely connected to star formation. We believe the filaments plays a crucial role in regulating star formation, while the processes of massive star and cluster formation in filaments are still less well understood. What we do are using molecular lines and dust continuum emissions to reveal the filaments properties, how it regulates embedded dense structures which form stars. I have developed a Python code to identify the filament in Position-Positon-Velovity (PPV) space using line emissions.

Turbulence

According to an apocryphal story, Werner Heisenberg was asked what he would ask God, given the opportunity. His reply was: "When I meet God, I am going to ask him two questions: Why relatively? And why turbulence? I really believe he will have an answer for the first." I led an ALMA project to measure the turbulent properties in molecular clouds. We have studied the turbulent properties toward a subregion (NGC 6334S) of my favor molecular cloud, NGC 6334 complex, with ALMA observations. We find that the gas non-thermal motions are predominantly subsonic or transonic toward NGC 6334S and its embedded dense cores.
Here is the paper about the turbulent properties toward NGC6334S:
Li et al 2020., "ALMA Observations of NGC 6334S. I. Forming Massive Stars and Clusters in Subsonic and Transonic Filamentary Clouds".

70 micro dark clumps

To complete the picture of massive star and cluster formation, we must understand their extreme early evolutionary stages. I led an SMA project to survey a sample of massive 70 micro dark clouds which represents the extreme early stage of massive star formation.
Here are some our papers about the 70 micro dark clumps:
(1) Li et al 2019., "Formation of Massive Protostellar Clusters—Observations of Massive 70 μm Dark Molecular Clouds".
(2) Li et al 2020., "ALMA Observations of NGC 6334S. I. Forming Massive Stars and Clusters in Subsonic and Transonic Filamentary Clouds".

Star Formation Feedback

Star formation feedbacks, in form of outflows, jets and radiations, are significantly changing the surrounding environments. I led a series of programs with serval telescopes to survey outflows at various evolutionary stages of massive star formation.
Here is our first paper of this survey project:
Li et al 2019., "A SiO J = 5 → 4 Survey Toward Massive Star Formation Regions".

Line survey

More than 300 molecules have been found in interstellar medium. The molecular lines are ideal probes of various gas environments. We have carried out a line survey toward 4 HII regions, which in different molecular clouds, in order to study the gas properties in different molecular clouds.
Here is the paper of this line survey:
Li et al 2017., "Millimetre spectral line mapping observations towards four massive star-forming H II regions".
